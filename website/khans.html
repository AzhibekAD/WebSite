Kerei khan (Kazakh: Керей хан; 1425—1474) one of the founders and the first khan of the Kazakh khanate. Great-grandchildren of Urus Khan. According to “Tavarih-i guzida-yi nusrat-name”, Kerey Khan. the only son of Bolat, the son of Toktakia. After the death of Barak Khan, a period of weakening began in Ak-Orda, which was taken advantage of by the Shibanids, who proclaimed the young Abulkhair khan. The strengthening of his power led to the displacement of the Ordaids (descendants of Orda Khan, great-grandchildren of Urus Khan) Kerey Khan and Janibek Khan to the areas of the Syrdarya River and the city of Karatau.

Zhanibek khan (Kazakh: Жәнібек хан)Founder of the Kazakh Khanate and the dynasty of Kazakh Khans, son of Barak Khan, great-grandson of Uly Khan. Lived in the 15th century. There is no reliable data on life and activities before 1450.In historical sources, the name of Zhanibek Khan is more common than Kerey Khan, due to various circumstances. In the works "Muizz Al-Ansab" written in 1426 and "Tau'ih-I guzida-Yi Nusrat-Namada" written in the first years of the XVI century, his name is often mentioned as one of the founders of the Kazakh Khanate in historical works such as "Tarikhi Rashidi", "Fath-name", "Shaibani-name". In the works of Abelgazy, Kadyrgali Zhalayri, Gaffari, Haidar Razi, the name of Zhanibek Khan appears as a representative of the ruling dynasty in Eastern Deshti-Kipchak.

Esim khan (Kazakh: Есім хан; 1598—1628) In Kazakh history, Esim Khan was known as "Yensegey boily er Esim", this title was awarded to him in 1598 for outstanding performance in a campaign to Maurennakhr with his brother Tauekel Khan. Esim Khan is the son of Shygai Khan, who previously lived in the Khan's horde of the Kazakh Khanate in Turkestan.In 1598-1628, Esim Khan, who took over the power of the country after his brother Tauekel Khan, ruled the Kazakh Horde. He lived and ruled in Turkestan, the capital of the Kazakh Khanate. During the reign of the Sultan, his brother Tauekel Khan included the land from Turkestan to Samarkand as part of the Kazakh Khanate. He stayed in Samarkand with 20 thousand troops, and Tauekel Khan went to Bukhara with about 80 thousand troops, but did not win.In 1598, after the death of Tauelsizdik, the Khan took the throne.

Tauke khan (1635-1715) - Khan of the younger zhuz since 1652, and since 1672-ruler of the Kazakh Khanate, son of Khan Salkam Zhangir. Her mother, Banu Hanim, is the daughter of the Ruler of the khoshouyt tribe of Kalmyk, Kunden-Taishi. When he inherited the reins of power of the Kazakh Khanate (1680), Tauke was a skilled man who came of age and stopped thinking, intervened in the affairs of the state and gained a lot of experience. So he took the throne and went on with his father's business, and although he pursued his policy, he did not leave the old path when it came to its implementation, but went on his own new path. Tauke is distinguished from other Kazakh Khans by the fact that he is a visionary leader, a wise reformer. At the same time, he adapts to his great ancestor Kasym Khan.
